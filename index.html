This is open Summer of code 2016

Open Summer of code is a 4 week programme in different Belgian cities during the summer of 2016. The goal: provide Belgian based students the training, network and support necessary to transform open innovation projects into powerful real-world services.

Check out previous editions of Summer of code below:

Brussels

Selor

18 — 28 July

Brussels

Proximus

Projects

Autofill

Autofill is a B2B solution that enables co-workers to carpool and commute together.

Data Science Lab

This is team Mobylink and their objective is to convert open data to linked data to make data more accessible.

Fedict

Fedict's team is working on an application for transforming WSDL (XML-language) files to DCAT (RDF vocabulary) files. This makes it easier for users to access related data. It utilises XSLT for this transformation.

My VIAA

A portal site where the partners of VIAA can access their services in a centralized place and see statistics about their usage and other data.

Peek-A-Book

Cultuurconnect challenges local libraries and cultural institutions to think about digital innovation. This team gets to create two proof of concepts (POCs).

Revit

A backoffice application for Selor to evaluate candidates based on certain metrics.

Spitsgids

Tired of not finding a seat on your commute? Spitsgids shows you whether your train will be busy or not. This information, together with delay and incident data, gets aggregated into a dashboard for Metrotime, which they can use on their website and in their newspaper.
